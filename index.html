Interesting Facts About Camel
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Interesting Facts About Chameleon
  • Unlike many lizards, chameleons cant't regrow their tails.
  • Their eyes can swivel around in two different directions simultaneously.
  • Skin crystals enable them to change color at will.

Interesting Facts About Pigeon
  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals. They are one of only species to pass the ‘mirror test’, which is a test of self recognition.They can also recognize each letter of the alphabet, differentiate between photographs, and can even distinguish different humans within a photograph.
  • Pigeons can travel 400-600 miles away from its home and can amazingly return within the day.
  • Amazingly pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.

Interesting Facts About Teddy Bear
  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

Interesting Facts About Cicada
  • The body of a cicada is similar to that of a violin or a guitar, in that much of it consists of empty, air-filled spaces that act like a resonating chamber and amplify the sound they generate.
  • Cicadas kind of ‘sweat’-They feed on plant sap, so they are constantly sucking in liquids, and as the temperature gets hotter and hotter, and they start to overheat, they remove the water from their blood and pass it through ducts in their body. As it exits their body through pores on their thorax, they get that continuous evaporative cooling.
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes
